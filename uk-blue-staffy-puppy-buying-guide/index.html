What are the Common Blue Staffy Health Issues?

While generally robust, common blue Staffy health issues can include:

L-2-HGA (L-2-Hydroxyglutaric Aciduria): A neurological disorder affecting coordination and gait.
HC-HSF4 (Hereditary Cataracts): An inherited eye condition leading to blindness.
Degenerative Myelopathy (DM): A progressive disease of the spinal cord.
Eye Conditions: Such as Distichiasis or Persistent Pupillary Membranes.
Skin Allergies: Staffies can be prone to environmental or food allergies.

🐾 15 Questions to Ask Any Blue Staffy Breeder Before You Buy

Print this checklist and use it on every breeder visit. An ethical breeder will welcome every question. A bad one will dodge them.

  1. Are both parents KC-registered? Ask to see the original KC registration certificates, not photocopies.
  2. Can I see the L-2-HGA and HC-HSF4 DNA test results? Both parents should be clear or carrier-to-clear (never carrier-to-carrier).
  3. Can I see the mother (dam) with the puppies? If the mother is not present, walk away. This is the single biggest red flag for a puppy farm.
  4. How many litters does the dam have per year? The KC recommends no more than 4 litters per dam lifetime. More than 2 per year is a warning sign.
  5. Have the puppies been vet-checked? Ask for the vet practice name and check date.
  6. What vaccinations have they received? Puppies should have at least their first vaccination before leaving at 8 weeks.
  7. Have they been microchipped? UK law requires microchipping before sale.
  8. What worming programme have they been on? Puppies should be wormed at 2, 5, and 8 weeks minimum.
  9. Do you offer a health guarantee or purchase contract? Reputable breeders provide written contracts covering hereditary conditions.
  10. Have the puppies been socialised with children, other dogs, and household noises? The critical socialisation window is 3-12 weeks.
  11. What food are they currently eating? A good breeder provides a feeding schedule and a supply of current food.
  12. Can I have references from previous buyers? Confident ethical breeders connect you with past puppy families.
  13. What happens if I can no longer keep the puppy? Ethical breeders will always take a puppy back rather than let it go to a rescue.
  14. Are you a member of the Staffordshire Bull Terrier Club? Club membership shows commitment to breed standards.
  15. Can I visit the puppies more than once before deciding? Any pressure to "buy now or lose your spot" is a manipulation tactic.

📋 Your First 30 Days with a Blue Staffy Puppy — Complete Checklist

The first month sets the foundation for your puppy's entire life. Follow this week-by-week timeline to get it right.

Week 1 — Settling In

  • Keep the house calm, limit visitors for the first 3 days
  • Set up crate in a quiet corner with a worn item of your clothing
  • Feed the same food as the breeder for at least 7 days
  • Establish a toilet routine, take outside every 2 hours and after meals
  • Register with a local vet within 48 hours
  • Book 2nd vaccination appointment (usually 10-12 weeks)

Week 2 — First Routines

  • Begin name recognition training (5-minute sessions, 3x daily)
  • Introduce "sit" using reward-based methods only
  • Start handling exercises for ears, paws, and mouth to prepare for vet visits
  • Carry puppy outdoors to experience sounds and smells before second vaccination
  • Contact insurance provider, cover should start Day 1

Week 3 — Socialisation Begins

  • Puppy vaccination complete, begin controlled outdoor walks (5 mins per month of age)
  • Introduce to vaccinated, friendly adult dogs
  • Expose to traffic, children, cyclists positively and at a distance
  • Book puppy training class (KC Good Citizen programme)
  • Start "leave it" and "come" commands

Week 4 — Building Confidence

  • First puppy training class attended
  • Introduce alone time, start with 5 minutes and build to 1 hour over the month
  • Weigh puppy and adjust food portions accordingly
  • Begin introducing car travel with short positive trips
  • Review toilet training progress, accidents indoors should be reducing
