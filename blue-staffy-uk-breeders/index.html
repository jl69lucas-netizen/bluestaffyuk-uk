Frequently Asked Questions: Your Peace of Mind with BlueStaffyUK

To help you feel completely confident in choosing a blue Staffy puppy from us, we’ve answered some of the most common questions about our ethical breeding practices, health protocols, and commitment as blue Staffy UK breeders.

What makes BlueStaffyUK an ethical blue Staffy breeder in Glasgow?

We prioritise the welfare, health, and temperament of our parent dogs above all else. This means comprehensive health testing for our Dam (Maggie) and Sire (Jones), KC registration, raising puppies in a loving home environment, and providing lifelong support to our families. We are dedicated to transparent and responsible practices.

Are your Staffordshire Bull Terrier puppies health-tested?

Absolutely. The health of our blue Staffies is paramount. Both Maggie and Jones are extensively health-tested and certified clear for L-2-HGA and HC-HSF4, ensuring the genetic well-being of our litters. You can find more details on our Staffy Health & Care Page.

What is the typical temperament of your blue Staffy puppies?

Our home-raised blue Staffy puppies are known for their loving, confident, and well-socialized temperaments. Thanks to early exposure to family life and our careful breeding, they are ideal family dogs UK, known for their loyalty and playful nature.

Can I get a blue Staffy puppy delivered to my home outside Glasgow?

We offer safe and reliable UK-wide puppy delivery using DEFRA-approved pet transport services. This means whether you’re looking for a blue Staffy puppy for sale in the UK, Cornwall, Coventry, or Essex, your new de-wormed Staffy pup can arrive at your doorstep comfortably and securely. Learn more about our process on our Delivery Information Page.

What should a first-time dog owner know before getting a blue Staffy?

For first-time dog owners, welcoming a blue Staffy is an exciting journey! Key considerations include committing to early socialization, consistent positive training, and providing plenty of exercise. We provide a comprehensive Puppy Care Guide and are always available for advice and support throughout your dog’s life, ensuring a smooth transition for both you and your new blue Staffy pup. Find helpful puppy care guidance from the RSPCA

What two breeds make a Staffy?

The Staffordshire Bull Terrier evolved in the 19th century in the Black Country of England, primarily from the crossing of the Bulldog and various terriers, particularly the Old English Terrier (now extinct). This combination resulted in a dog with strength, tenacity, and agility. See History and characteristics of the Staffordshire Bull Terrier
